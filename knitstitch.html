Pitch

Knitstitch is a tablet app for Knit N Crochet 4 a Better World members and instructors to share and start knitting projects together. This app was a proposal for Knit N Crochet, a knitting club in the Richmond Public Library that met once a month to knit and crochet projects for fun or to donate.

Team: Helen Bayogos

Role: User Interface Designer, Prototyper, User Experience Designer, Camera Girl

Tools: Adobe Creative Suite, Axure RP, Google Docs

Project Details

The core business problem Knit N Crochet had was that it was hard for members to learn or teach someone a technique in the one hour monthly meeting. Many members would have unfinished projects and some would lose motivation to finish projects when they were at home. Our objective was to enhance the social aspect of the club outside of their meetings.

Role

I was mostly in charge of user interface (UI) and prototyping for this project. This included design of the app layout and content, as well as creating the tablet prototype. I also assisted in some visual design elements, such as creating the logo and mockup posters.

Tablet Design


Login Page

Creating an account was included to keep track of projects and let others know who you are.


Home Page

A block grid was used to showcase the projects and the status of others on that project.


Notifications

The notification sidebar included in-progress projects and upcoming knitting sessions.


Project Page

Details of the knitting project and other people's status made it easier to see who was working on the same project.


Chat Screen

If anyone had trouble or forgot techniques, they could get help by messaging others that are in progress or completed the project.


Update Project Status

After finishing the project, the user could upload a picture of their finished item and update their status.

Cultural Probes

To get to know the client better, we created crafted activites for them to complete in order to get to know them better as instructors and their love for knitting. I proposed many of the ideas for the activites and created all the instruction tags.

Concept Posters

Early on in the process, we narrowed our concepts down to three that we could carry forward with. We ultimately chose Knitipedia because the concept seemed to fit Knit N Crochet's core need better than the rest.

Journey Frameworks & Storyboards

For each concept, we created journey frameworks and storyboards to see how feasible each idea was. I created the layout and content for the journey frameworks.

Logo

Creating the logo was one of my most memorable experiences from this project. It got me thinking on who Knit N Crochet was and what it stood for.

A sense of community and familiarity were my inspirations behind the logo.

Reflection

Researching, setting constraints, and defining a core business problem were at the heart of this project. We were able to target a market and offer a solution that would help both business and customer.

Through this project, I grew an interest in user experience design and it has helped me to think critically about every decision that is made. In my visual design role, I used this thinking to make design choices and iterations more efficiently to ensure the best product was produced.
