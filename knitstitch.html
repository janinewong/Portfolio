Knitstitch

Knit N Crochet 4 Better World needed a design intervention for their knitting club which only meets once a month in the Richmond Public Library. We gave them just that and more.

Timeline

Jan 2015 - Apr 2015

Objective

We saw the need to enhance the social experience between the members of Knit N Crochet since they only meet once a month. Our proposal to them: Knitstitch, an iPad application letting them share and start knitting projects together as long as they had wifi!

Strategy

Our first goal: getting to know Knit N Crochet like the back of our hand. We created cultural probes in their hand-crafted style to connect with them. We then explored different ways to solve a problem Knit N Crochet had through three concept posters and journey frameworks. Having narrowed down the concept to one, we implemented Knitstitch - a medium fidelity prototype on the iPad. Finally, we created a promotional video which summarizes our concept.

Cultural Probes

Our goal was to get to know Knit N Crochet on a personal level. We did this by giving them a handmade package of goodies to complete and return to us. Some of the activities inside of the box asked personal questions such as their passions, goals, and close circle of friends. Each activity related back to the values or interests of Knit N Crochet.

In this section, I created the small instruction placards for each activity (e.g. Scratch-n-Capture). Also, I came up with creative names for each activity.

Concept Posters

To get our team thinking, we came up with three posters that displayed different ideas for solving a problem Knit N Crochet had. It needed to get the point across quickly with minimal text and a strong icon.

In this section, I designed the layout and wrote the content. My partner created the icons for each concept.

Journey Frameworks

The next step was putting ourselves in Knit N Crochet's shoes. One huge problem they had was keeping their social interaction among members strong, especially for newcomers. Knitting beginners needed more help and resources from Knit N Crochet teachers outside of their meeting times to finish their knit projects.

For all journey frameworks, I designed the layouts and majority of the content. My partner worked on the sketches and we both combined our efforts for the interaction.

Ipad Prototype

Knitstitch is live on both a desktop and iPad version. However, be warned -- don't expect everything to work! It's only a medium fidelity prototype, but feel free to check it out. P.S. Follow the blinking buttons to know where to click next!

I created the entire iPad application on Axure RP, a wireframing prototytpe program. Also, I created the logo and interactions on the iPad, while my partner helped with page layouts and graphic elements.

Promotional Video

Our final step: create a video to showcase our design. This video pretty much sums up the problem and solution we gave, so I highly encourage watching if you didn't follow along with everything I've said so far.

Since my partner was better at video editing, she designed the video storyline and script. I was more her assistant, cutting video footage and operating the camera minimally.

Conclusion

More than anything, I gained the most experience in branding and graphic design. Most of my tasks included reflecting Knit N Crochet in all activities and the iPad application. I learned that knowing the user is the most important step before you can create any design. After all, great design follows after knowing your audience well.
