Rebranding Misa

As the Vice President of Marketing in the Management Information Systems Association (MISA), I rebranded MISA to be more professional in terms of design and content.

Team: Candy Leong, Kevin Lee, Esther Gutman, Louie Kwan, Jacky Yu, William Fong

Role: Marketing, Graphic Design

Tools: Wordpress, HTML/CSS, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator

Objective

MISA is a Business club at Simon Fraser University (SFU) specializing in management information systems and with over 100+ members. After being hired as the Vice President of Marketing, my year long goal was to rebrand MISA into a trustworthy and professional club through MISA's website and social media platforms.

Redesigned Website

The MISA website before my term was very outdated -- sorry, no pictures were taken beforehand, but trust me, it lacked constant upkeep and any sense of branding! It was run on WordPress, so I chose a theme called Adamos. I used a combination of html, css, and WordPress widgets to redesign the theme to fit MISA, while still keeping the old brand's colors and logo to ensure familiarity among its current members. P.S. Just a warning that since my term is over, the designs starting after May 2015 and before April 2014 are not mine!

The Misa Mixer

Rebranding the 8th annual Misa Mixer was a challenging, but fulfilling experience. The Misa Mixer is a semi-formal event connecting students to industry professionals in the Information Technology (IT) sector. I designed the logo and all promotional materials including: banners, small posters for sharing on social media, nametags, and booklets. Each small poster was designed to give snippets of information on companies that were attending with 10 companies in total. The nametags were color coordinated and labeled by professionals, students, executives, photographers, and keynote speakers for easier recognition when mingling. Lastly, the event booklet was passed out among attendees and showed event information.

BC AWARE: Protect Yourself

The BC Aware workshop was sponsored by ISACA, a information technology company stressing the importance of data security. It's purpose was to increase awareness of security issues in technology and connect IT professionals with students. I led a team of myself and two coordinators to organize the event, while simultaneously creating promotional materials.
