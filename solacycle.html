Objective

Solacycle is a fictitious high-end bicycle company selling custom made bikes for city dwellers. For this project, we had to create a responsive website for the company.

Team: Ocean Zou

Role: Web Developer, User Interface Designer, Visual Designer

Tools: Adobe Creative Suite, Github, HTML Text Editor

Project Details

Although this was a company we made up, we had to research the target market and competitors in the industry. We targeted casual city bikers who wanted a bike that fit their needs, whether that be speed, comfort, or performance. Our design decisions focused on making Solacycle like a biking companion, helping users choose the bike that resembled them.

Role

I was in charge of designing and coding the layouts of the "About," "Contact Us," "Shop Bikes," and "Build Bikes" pages. My partner and I discussed the general layouts together and then we coded our designated pages separately.

Website Features

One of the main features of the website was customizing the bikes on the "Build Bikes" page. Users could customize their frames, seats, handles, and tires to their liking. Instant feedback was a must, so I designed a 4 step process with pictures. When the customer chose their preferred options, the bike would update to the option chosen.

Another feature implemented was a bar rating the comfort, speed, and performance. This helped users who wanted to quickly know the general information about the bike without reading the entire text. Users could then add the bike to their cart or choose to customize the preset bike's features.

Graphics

I used a different approach to the custom bike progress bar by creating a bike icon and highlighting the current item the user is customizing.

I kept the logo simple with a splash of orange color for consistent branding and added a bicycle wheel inside the "O" of Solacycle.

Reflection

Throughout this project, I improved the most in designating work load because it took a lot of coordination and communication with my partner to make our separate web pages look like one style.

If I could do this project again, I would focus the design more on the bike images to create a brand image of quality which we were trying to achieve.
