Pitch

Access is a mobile app paired with an online website designed for American Express cardholders to access a premium tier of their favourite events and experiences.

Team: Melinda Ng, Bruce Beh, Justin Poon, Sid Zhao, Tobey Chen

Role: Visual Designer, User Interface (UI) Designer

Tools: Adobe Creative Suite, Flinto, Sketch, PowerPoint, Google Docs

Project Details

This app was a proposal for extending American Express's existing Membership Experiences program. The target market was affluent individuals who already go to events such as concerts or fine dining, but would pay extra to get premium experiences such as VIP tickets or exclusive seating. Access would recommend other events or experiences to customers based on their card usage and redefine what it is like to be an AmEx cardholder.

Business Problem

Since it has become a standard for credit cards to offer spend-based benefits, American Express does not offer value for customers to keep AMEX cards as opposed to their competitors. In order to keep current cardholders, we focused on highlighting AMEX's membership experiences so that it was more well-known and created a more memorable digital experience.

My Role as a Visual & Interface Designer

I was mostly in charge of the visual and interface design for this project. This included design of the mobile/website layout and content, presentation slides, process book, typography, and colour. I worked closely with the interaction designer/prototyper to make sure the mobile app design was consistent with animations.

Interactions


Minimize Interaction

I suggested the idea of minimizing the title to stick to the top when scrolling for easier scrolling.


Ticket Design

I designed the ticket to correspond with the folding animation so it was delightful for cardholders to show their passes.


Sticky Interaction

I suggested for the "Buy" button and info to be stuck to the top upon scrolling for easier access after reading.

Website Design

New Design

Sample Event Page

Scroll Interaction

Iterations

In the preliminary stages of the app, I designed two colour themes: white and black. Ultimately, my team and I decided black and gold fit the premium theme we were looking for.

For the app's navigation bar, I focused on contrast with the icon's color and bold typography to make reading easier.

I focused on designing a ticket page that still resembled a ticket, but wasn't so flat. The extra animation made the design pop out.


Iteration

Reflection

Researching, setting constraints, and defining a core business problem were at the heart of this project. We were able to target a market and offer a solution that would help both business and customer.

Through this project, I grew an interest in user experience design and it has helped me to think critically about every decision that is made. In my visual design role, I used this thinking to make design choices and iterations more efficiently to ensure the best product was produced.
